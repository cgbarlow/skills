Source of truth: IR3G (March 2026). Rates and thresholds shown here are for the year ended **31 March 2026** only — they will change for later years.

All amounts are NZD. Round whole numbers per IRD's convention; show cents in calculation working only.

## 2. ACC earners' levy (2026)

- **Rate:** 1.67% (0.0167) of liable earnings
- **Maximum liable earnings:** $152,790
- **Maximum levy:** $2,551.59

### Worksheet — ACC earners' levy (only needed if amending SOI)

```
5.  Total PAYE deducted                                    → Box 11A        $______
6.  Box 4 (the levy)                                                        $______
7.  Box 5 − Box 6 = total tax deducted                     → Box 11E        $______
```

### Income NOT liable for ACC earners' levy

NZ Super, income-tested benefits, non-taxable allowances, student allowances, veteran's pension, living-alone payments, redundancy payments, retiring allowances, jury/witness fees, interest and dividends, taxable Māori authority distributions, free or discounted ESS shares, partnership income earned by a non-working partner, pensions from non-FMA-registered superannuation schemes, overseas pensions, rents, estate/trust income, royalties, attributed PIE income, foreign superannuation withdrawals.

## 3. Independent Earner Tax Credit (IETC) (2026)

- **Eligible income range:** $24,000 – $70,000 net income (Box 30)
- **Maximum annual credit:** $520
- **Abatement:** 13 cents per dollar above $66,000
- **Eligibility per month requires:** NZ tax resident, AND not entitled to Working for Families (or overseas equivalent), AND did not receive main benefit / NZ Super / veteran's pension / overseas equivalent. Even one disqualifying day in a month disqualifies the whole month.

### Worksheet — IETC

```
A.  Income (from Box 32 of return):                                $______
    If A > $70,000 → IETC = $0; STOP.
    If A < $24,000 → IETC = $0; STOP.

E.  Number of eligible months (count whole months Apr–Mar):        ____
F.  D × E:                                                         $______
G.  F ÷ 12 = IETC entitlement                              → Box 33 $______
```

## 6. Student loan end-of-year repayment (2026)

- **Annual repayment threshold:** $24,128
- **Repayment rate:** 12% (0.12) on income above threshold
- **Triggers obligation:** adjusted net income ≥ $500 AND total income (incl. salary/wages) ≥ $24,128

If the user must complete an IR215 (Adjust your income), do **not** use this worksheet — IRD will calculate the obligation after they file the IR3 + IR215.

## 7. Provisional tax for 2027 — Standard option (S)

Default option if RIT > $5,000.

```
1.  RIT (Box 36A):                                                    $______
2.  Box 1 × 0.05:                                                     $______
3.  Box 1 + Box 2 = 2027 provisional tax              → Box 39B       $______
    Print "S" in Box 39A.
    Each instalment = Box 3 ÷ 3, due:
        • 28 August 2026
        • 15 January 2027
        • 7 May 2027
```

If the user expects 2027 income to be lower, offer the **Estimation option (E)**: estimate 2027 taxable income, work out tax on that, subtract estimated 2027 credits/PAYE, divide by 3. Warn that under-estimation can attract use-of-money interest and penalties.

If GST-registered and qualifying, **Ratio option (R)** may apply — refer to IR289.
